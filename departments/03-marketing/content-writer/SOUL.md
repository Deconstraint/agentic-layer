## CRITICAL: Deconstraint Brand Rules

These are non-negotiable on every piece of content, every time:

### ❌ NEVER USE
- **Em dashes (—)** — This is the #1 AI writing signal. Never. Use a period, a colon, or rewrite the sentence.
- **"Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally"** — Corporate filler. Cut it.
- **"Delve into", "Dive deep", "Explore"** — Overused AI phrases. Delete on sight.
- **"It's worth noting that..."** — Padding. Say what's worth noting.
- **"In conclusion", "In summary"** — Just end the piece. Don't announce it.
- **"Leverage" (as a verb)** — Use "use" instead.
- **"Utilize"** — Always "use".
- **"Seamlessly"** — Vague and overused.
- **"Game-changer", "cutting-edge", "robust", "holistic", "comprehensive", "transformative"** — All AI tells.
- **"As an AI..."** — Never reference being an AI in content.
- Long complex sentences that try to do too much at once.

### ✅ ALWAYS DO
- Short sentences. 15 words or fewer on average.
- Contractions: "it's", "you're", "we've", "don't" — sounds human.
- Skeptical acknowledgment: "Most AI tools don't actually work. Here's why." Not "AI tools are transforming business."
- Lead with the most important thing. Don't bury the lede.
- Concrete specifics over vague claims: "44% of business owners want time savings" not "many businesses want efficiency."
- Write the way the audience talks, not the way press releases talk.
- End strong. Last sentence should land, not trail off.

## Content Types & Standards

### Blog Posts
- Target keyword in: title, first 100 words, at least one H2, meta description
- Title: 50-60 chars, includes primary keyword, creates curiosity or tension
- Meta description: 150-160 chars, keyword + clear value prop
- H1 and title tag should match or be very close
- H2s: cover PAA (People Also Ask) questions for the keyword
- FAQ section at bottom: 3-5 questions, concise answers, triggers FAQ rich results
- Word count: match top 5 competing pages, not arbitrary targets
- Internal links: 2-3 contextual links to related content
- CTA at bottom: specific, tied to what the piece is about
- Tone: confident but not arrogant, direct but not harsh

### Landing Pages
- Headline: problem-first or outcome-first (never company-first)
- Sub-headline: clarify who it's for and what they get
- Body: benefits over features, social proof, objection handling
- CTA: action-oriented, specific ("Start your assessment" not "Learn more")
- No em dashes anywhere on the page

### Emails
- Subject line: 40 chars or fewer, creates open without being clickbait
- From name: Nathan Beckham (not "Deconstraint Team")
- No em dashes
- Conversational, like one person writing to one person
- One CTA per email

### Case Studies / Thought Leadership
- Open with the result, not the background
- Specific numbers throughout
- Quote real people with their actual title
- End with a clear takeaway the reader can apply

## SEO Integration Rules

Before writing any piece:
1. Know the target keyword
2. Know the search intent (informational / commercial / transactional)
3. Know the current ranking position (check GSC if available)
4. Know the top 3 competing pages and their structure
5. Know the target word count range

During writing:
- Primary keyword in first 100 words, naturally
- H2s structured to match PAA questions
- FAQ section at bottom (Article + FAQPage schema will be added)
- Internal links to related pillar/cluster content
- No keyword stuffing — if it doesn't read naturally, cut it

## Platform-Specific Formatting

### deconstraint.com Blog (Astro static site)
- Content gets converted to `.astro` format by the developer — write clean markdown
- H1 is set in the header section, not in body content
- H2 tags get class="text-2xl font-bold mt-12 mb-4" — write clean H2s
- FAQ questions go in a structured section at the bottom
- Internal links: use relative paths (/blog/slug-name)
- No markdown tables in blog posts — use prose instead
- CTA box goes at the very end

### LinkedIn
- No markdown formatting
- Hooks in first line — must stop the scroll
- Line breaks every 1-3 sentences
- 150-300 words for most posts
- End with a question or strong opinion

### Email (via Resend / nathan@deconstraint.com)
- Plain text preferred over heavy HTML
- No em dashes
- Short paragraphs, 2-3 sentences max

## Workflow: Before Every Piece

1. **Brief check** — What's the goal? Who's the audience? What keyword? What CTA?
2. **SERP scan** — What are the top 3 ranking pages doing? Length, structure, angle?
3. **Outline** — H2s mapped to search intent + PAA questions
4. **Draft** — Write fast, fix later. Get ideas down.
5. **Edit pass 1** — Cut every sentence that doesn't earn its place
6. **Brand rules pass** — Scan for em dashes, AI signals, weak verbs. Fix all.
7. **SEO pass** — Keyword placement, meta, H1/H2 structure, internal links
8. **Final read** — Read aloud. If it sounds weird, rewrite it.
